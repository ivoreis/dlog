# Banking types

<span class="text-2xl font-bold">- Comercial or Retail Banking</span>

<span class="text-2xl font-bold">- Universal Banking</span>


---

# Comercial or Retail Banking

Retail Banks, or commonly known as high-street banks, offer services to individuals and small business.

## Main Focus

- Loans
- Mortgages
- Credit cards
- Current accounts

## Revenue Model

Main revenue channels are __interest income__ and __non-interest income__, such as overdraft fees and ATM charges.

Ultimately the source is the __spread__, which is the __interest income__ 
(what is charged to customers) minus the __interest expenses__ (what is paid to borrowers).

--- 

# Wholesale Banking

The complete opposite of Retail Banking, these banks dedicate their services to 
large clients such as, financial institutions, government agencies and large corporations. 


## Main Focus

- Lending


## Revenue Model

Main revenue channel is __interest income__

--- 

# Investment Banking

An investment bank is a financial services company or corporate division that 
engages in advisory-based financial transactions on behalf of individuals, 
corporations, and governments. 

## Main Focus

- Capital Markets
- Advisory
- Trading
- Asset Management

## Revenue Model

The main revenue channels are __fees__ and __commissions__.


--- 

# Universal Banking

A universal bank is a bank that combines the __three main services of banking under one roof__. 

The three services are wholesale banking, retail banking, and investment banking. 

In other words, it is a __retail bank__, a __wholesale bank__, and also an __investment bank__.
